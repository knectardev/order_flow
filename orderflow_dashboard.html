PHAT CANDLES
Body shading (imbalance gate)
  • Neutral (no P/b split) Translucent green or red body (swatches: up · down), matching chart neutral candles with no two-tone split. When |topNorm − botNorm| is below the gate, or when imbalance is high but opposes bar direction (disagreement) — the chart keeps a single neutral body (flow vs price conflict, not a separate two-tone visual).
  • P-shapeUp candle · darker top half — buyers pressed the upper body
  • b-shapeDown candle · darker bottom half — sellers pressed the lower body
Body width (volume · visible chart)
  • Lower vol
    Higher vol
    Narrow vs wide PHAT bodies map bar volume to width using the min and max volume among bars currently visible on the price chart (linear scale, with a floor so bodies never vanish). A bar near the bottom of that range looks narrow; near the top looks wide. Panning or zooming changes the visible window, so min/max shift and the same historical bar can look wider or narrower — that trades cross-pan stability for legibility of what’s on screen. Bodies ≤ one price tick still use the tighter width tier before volume is applied.
Wicks (on chart)
  • Geometry & strokes Upper and lower wicks run from the body to the bar’s high and low at a uniform line weight. Each wick meets the body on the edge that matches sweep order—which extreme printed first—which is why the two wicks often look asymmetric.
