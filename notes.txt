

### 1. The Strategy Logic Shift (The "Feature, Not a Bug")
There is a fundamental difference between your current logic and the proposed one:
* **Current (Close):** You are fading the move *within* the signal bar.
* **Next-Bar (Open):** You are betting that the reversal *persists* into the next bar.

**The "Gotcha":** If your `value_edge_reject` logic relies on the wick of the signal bar to provide the mean reversion, shifting to the Open of the next bar might actually **kill the strategy logic entirely**, not just reduce the profitability.
* **Recommendation:** When you implement this, **do not discard your "Close-based" results.** Keep them as a baseline. When you run the new "Open-based" logic, if the strategy collapses, you aren't necessarily failing an integrity test—you might just be discovering that your strategy *requires* near-instantaneous execution to function. That is an important finding in itself.

### 2. The "Gap" Execution Risk
In your plan, you mention using `open_price` of the next bar. 
* **The Risk:** ES futures often gap between bars (e.g., overnight or due to news). If you fire a signal at the close of Bar $N$, and the next bar opens with a massive gap against your position, your `open_price` entry might be significantly worse than the `close_price` of the previous bar.
* **Refinement:** Add a **"Gap Guard"** to your implementation. If `abs(open_next_bar - close_signal_bar) > slippage_threshold`, consider it an "invalid execution" and skip the trade. This makes the backtest much more realistic for live trading, where you wouldn't want to get "gapped" into a position.
